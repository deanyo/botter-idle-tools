Every way an item can roll, in one place. When the dungeon spawns a piece of loot, it walks through several rolls — rarity, quality, recolor, enchant, meta-rarity. Each roll is independent, and most stack. This page shows what the tooltip looks like at every stop along the way, so you can read the same fields the player sees.

Drop-rate numbers come from data/drop_tuning.json + the per-item rarity table. Tooltips here use the same color logic as the in-game widget, so a Legendary Vampire's Tooth · Ancient here looks like the one that drops in a dungeon.

Approximate drop rates (out of 1000 drops)

1 · Base rarity roll

Every drop rolls one of five rarities. Rarity decides the affix count (Common = 0, Uncommon = 1, Rare = 2, Epic = 3, Legendary = 4) and tints the tooltip border. Junk-tier commons make up the bulk of drops; legendaries are floor-tier-gated (T1 and T2 floors can't roll one).

2 · Quality (rusted → masterwork)

Independent of rarity. Multiplies the item's base stats by 0.80 → 1.20 in 20 steps. Most rolls land near the middle (Standard / Polished); extreme tails are rare. A common Rusted Dagger has the lowest possible damage; a legendary Sublime Demon Blade has the highest.

3 · Recolor (per-instance look)

~30% of drops roll a hue shift. Tinier slices roll Shimmer (~0.5%), Inverted (~0.1%), Prismatic (~0.05%) — the rarest and shiniest. Recolors carry a small stat lean (red → strength, blue → regen, etc). Items can author a permanent recolor via the item editor's "Look" section, in which case every drop renders that way.

4 · Enchant — bonus combat tag

Each item has its own enchant chance (0% on commons, up to 100% on flavor-defining uniques). On a hit, one bonus tag rolls from the item's possible-tags pool: vampiric, fire, cold, holy, dark, etc. These stack on top of the item's always-on tags.

5 · Enchant combo (rare double-roll)

A second enchant roll fires after the first hits, gated by enchant.combo_chance (~3%). If the resulting pair has a registered combo (e.g. fire + cold = Bloodfire), both flavors are replaced by the compound name with a unique color and effect. ~21 combo recipes shipped today.

6 · Meta-rarity (Ancient · Primal)

Independent of every other roll. Ancient (~1.1%) bumps every base + bonus stat by +20% and adds a gold tint to the tooltip header. Primal (~0.1%) — the rarest stamp in the game — bumps stats by +50% and renders deep red. Stacks with legendary + double-enchant + recolor for endgame chase items.

7 · The full stack

What it looks like when every roll lands in the player's favor. Realistically, the chance of seeing one of these in a single playthrough is ~1 in 50,000 drops — the chase reward.
